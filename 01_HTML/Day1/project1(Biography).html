Biography of Nicola Tesla

Early Life

Nikola Tesla was born on July 10, 1856, in Smiljan, part of the Austrian Empire (modern-day Croatia). He showed early signs of brilliance, particularly in mathematics and physics, and pursued studies in engineering and physics at the Austrian Polytechnic in Graz and later at the Charles University in Prague.

Career

Tesla moved to the United States in 1884, where he briefly worked with Thomas Edison before branching out on his own. He became a pioneering inventor, primarily known for his contributions to electrical engineering. Tesla's work on alternating current (AC) power systems revolutionized electricity distribution, making long-distance power transmission possible.

Achievements

Tesla invented and patented numerous technologies, including the Tesla coil, induction motors, and wireless transmission systems. His rivalry with Edison, known as the "War of Currents," saw Tesla's AC system eventually adopted as the standard for electric power distribution.

Legacy

Tesla's visionary work laid the foundation for modern electrical systems, wireless communication, and even concepts that would later lead to radar and X-rays. Though he died in relative obscurity in 1943, his inventions continue to shape technology, and today he is celebrated as one of the greatest inventors in history.
